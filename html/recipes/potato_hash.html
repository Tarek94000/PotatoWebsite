Classic Potato Hash

Servings: 4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

This classic potato hash is a versatile and hearty dish, perfect for breakfast or brunch. It's easy to make and packed with flavor, thanks to the combination of crispy potatoes, onions, and bell peppers.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Russet or Yukon Gold) 2 pounds, peeled and diced
Olive oil 3 tablespoons
Onion, chopped 1 large
Bell peppers, chopped 2 (any color)
Garlic, minced 2 cloves
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Fresh parsley, chopped (optional) 2 tablespoons
  1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the diced potatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, until they start to brown and become crispy, about 10-12 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped onion and bell peppers to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Stir in the minced garlic, salt, and black pepper. Cook for another minute.
  5. If using, sprinkle with fresh parsley before serving.
  6. Serve hot as a side dish or top with fried eggs for a complete meal.

Did you know? Potato hash was a popular dish during the American Gold Rush. Miners, known for their long days and limited resources, relied on this hearty meal made from leftover meat and potatoes. It was easy to prepare, filling, and could be cooked over an open fire, making it a staple in their diet and a symbol of resourcefulness!
