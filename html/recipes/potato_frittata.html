Classic Potato Frittata

Servings: 4 people

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 25 minutes

This classic potato frittata is a versatile and delicious dish that's perfect for breakfast, brunch, or even dinner. It's easy to make and can be customized with your favorite vegetables and cheeses.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet) 2 large, peeled and diced
Olive oil 2 tablespoons
Onion, chopped 1 medium
Bell pepper, chopped 1 medium
Eggs 6 large
Milk 1/4 cup
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Cheddar cheese, grated 1/2 cup
Fresh parsley, chopped (optional) 2 tablespoons
  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large oven-safe skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  3. Add the diced potatoes and cook until they start to soften, about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper to the skillet and cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes more.
  5. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, salt, and black pepper. Pour the egg mixture over the vegetables in the skillet.
  6. Cook on the stovetop until the edges begin to set, about 5 minutes.
  7. Sprinkle the grated cheddar cheese over the top and transfer the skillet to the preheated oven. Bake until the frittata is fully set and golden brown, about 10-15 minutes.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before slicing. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.
  9. Serve warm and enjoy!

Did you know? The potato frittata, a beloved Italian dish, was historically a way to repurpose leftovers. In Italian kitchens, no food was wasted, and the frittata became a perfect vehicle for turning yesterday's dinner into a delicious meal. This versatile dish highlights the Italian ingenuity in creating tasty, sustainable food from humble ingredients!
