Potato Gratin

Servings: 6 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour

This classic Potato Gratin is a rich and creamy side dish that's perfect for special occasions. Layers of thinly sliced potatoes baked in a creamy sauce with a golden, cheesy top.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet) 2 pounds
Heavy cream 2 cups
Garlic, minced 2 cloves
Gruyère cheese, grated 1 cup
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Fresh thyme, chopped (optional) 1 tablespoon
  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Peel the potatoes and slice them thinly (about 1/8 inch thick).
  3. In a small saucepan, heat the heavy cream with the minced garlic until warm (do not boil). Add salt, pepper, and fresh thyme if using.
  4. In a greased baking dish, layer half of the sliced potatoes. Pour half of the cream mixture over the potatoes and sprinkle with half of the grated cheese.
  5. Repeat with the remaining potatoes, cream mixture, and cheese.
  6. Cover the baking dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 45 minutes.
  7. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the top is golden and the potatoes are tender.
  8. Let the gratin cool for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

Did you know? Potato gratin, also known as gratin dauphinois, was first created in the Dauphiné region of France in the 18th century. Legend has it that this creamy, cheesy dish was served at a banquet hosted by the Duke of Clermont-Tonnerre in 1788, quickly becoming a favorite among French nobility and eventually spreading worldwide as a symbol of indulgent comfort food!
