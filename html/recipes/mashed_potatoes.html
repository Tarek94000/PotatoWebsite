Classic Mashed Potatoes

Servings: 4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Nothing complements a hearty main dish better than a creamy, buttery bowl of mashed potatoes. This classic recipe brings out the best in your spuds, ensuring a deliciously smooth and flavorful side dish perfect for any occasion.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Russet or Yukon Gold) 2 pounds
Salted butter 4 tablespoons
Heavy cream 1/2 cup
Salt 1 teaspoon
Freshly ground black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into quarters. Rinse under cold water.
  2. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Over low heat, gently shake the pot to dry the potatoes for about 1 minute.
  4. Mash the potatoes using a potato masher or a hand mixer on low speed, gradually adding butter and heavy cream until the mixture is smooth and creamy.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve hot with additional butter if desired.
  • Roast chicken or turkey
  • Grilled or roasted beef
  • Vegetarian options like mushroom gravy or vegetable stew
  • Grilled fish or seafood

Did you know? Mashed potatoes date back to 1771 when they were first mentioned in a French cookbook. They have since become a staple in many cuisines around the world, beloved for their creamy texture and versatility!
