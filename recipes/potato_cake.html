Potato Cakes

Servings: 4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

Golden, crispy potato cakes are a delicious way to use up leftover mashed potatoes. Perfect as a side dish or a light meal, these cakes are easy to make and incredibly satisfying.

Ingredient Amount
Mashed potatoes 2 cups
All-purpose flour 1/2 cup
Green onions, chopped 1/4 cup
Egg 1 large
Salt 1/2 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Vegetable oil For frying
  1. In a large bowl, combine mashed potatoes, flour, green onions, egg, salt, and black pepper. Mix until well combined.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  3. Form the potato mixture into patties, about 1/2 inch thick.
  4. Fry the patties in the hot oil until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  5. Transfer the potato cakes to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with additional green onions if desired.

Did you know? Potato cakes, also known as boxty in Ireland, have a rich history dating back to the Irish famine of the 1840s. These cakes were made to stretch meager food supplies, combining grated and mashed potatoes to create a hearty, filling dish. Boxty even inspired a traditional Irish rhyme: "Boxty on the griddle, boxty in the pan, if you can't make boxty, you'll never get a man!"
