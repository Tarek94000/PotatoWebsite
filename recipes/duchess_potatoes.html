Duchess Potatoes

Servings: 6 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Duchess potatoes are an elegant and delicious way to serve mashed potatoes. These beautifully piped, golden-brown potatoes are perfect for special occasions.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Yukon Gold or Russet) 2 pounds, peeled and quartered
Butter 1/2 cup
Heavy cream 1/4 cup
Egg yolks 3 large
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Nutmeg (optional) 1/4 teaspoon
  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place the potatoes in a large pot of salted water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes. Drain well.
  3. While the potatoes are still hot, pass them through a potato ricer or mash them until smooth.
  4. In a saucepan, heat the butter and heavy cream until the butter is melted. Stir this mixture into the mashed potatoes until well combined.
  5. Mix in the egg yolks, one at a time, until fully incorporated. Add salt, pepper, and nutmeg (if using).
  6. Transfer the potato mixture to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip. Pipe the potatoes into swirls or rosettes onto the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the potatoes are golden brown.
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Did you know? Duchess potatoes, originating from France, are mashed potatoes piped into fancy shapes and baked until golden. These elegant potatoes were first served to French royalty, making them a truly regal side dish!
