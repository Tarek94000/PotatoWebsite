Classic Potato Salad

Servings: 6 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 10 minutes

This classic potato salad recipe is a must-have for any summer gathering or BBQ. With tender potatoes, crunchy vegetables, and a creamy dressing, it's a crowd-pleaser that everyone will love.

Ingredient Amount
Potatoes (Yukon Gold or Red) 2 pounds
Mayonnaise 1 cup
Celery, chopped 1 cup
Red onion, finely chopped 1/4 cup
Dill pickles, chopped 1/4 cup
Mustard 1 tablespoon
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black pepper 1/2 teaspoon
Fresh dill, chopped (optional) 2 tablespoons
  1. Peel the potatoes and cut them into bite-sized pieces. Rinse under cold water.
  2. Place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a simmer and cook until the potatoes are tender, about 10 minutes.
  3. Drain the potatoes and let them cool slightly.
  4. In a large bowl, mix together mayonnaise, celery, red onion, dill pickles, mustard, salt, and pepper.
  5. Gently fold the potatoes into the dressing mixture until evenly coated. Add fresh dill if desired.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving to allow the flavors to meld.

Did you know? Potato salad can trace its roots back to 16th century Europe when Spanish explorers brought potatoes back from the Americas. Originally, potatoes were mixed with wine, vinegar, and spices, and served cold. This dish evolved into the creamy, mayonnaise-based versions we love today, making it a fascinating blend of history and flavor!
